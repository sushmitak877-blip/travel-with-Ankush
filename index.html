🌉 Lakshman Jhula

🌉 Lakshman Jhula

Rishikesh, Uttarakhand

About

Lakshman Jhula is a famous suspension bridge built over the Ganges River in Rishikesh. It is around 450 feet long and offers a beautiful scenic view.

Key Highlights

  • 450 feet long suspension bridge at Lakshman Jhula
  • Stunning panoramic views of the Ganges River
  • One of the most famous landmarks in Rishikesh
  • Surrounded by temples, ashrams, and vibrant cafes
  • Ideal spot for photography, especially during sunrise and sunset
  • A peaceful spiritual atmosphere attracting pilgrims and tourists
  • Walking across the bridge offers a unique and thrilling experience

Mythological Significance

It is believed that Lakshmana, the younger brother of Lord Rama, crossed the sacred Ganges River at this spot using a simple rope bridge during the time of the Ramayana. Because of this legendary event, the place came to be known as Lakshman Jhula. Even today, it is considered a spiritually significant site, attracting devotees and tourists who wish to connect with this ancient story and its divine importance.

Current Status

The bridge is currently closed due to safety concerns. Tourists use nearby bridges like Ram Jhula.
